Quick Timeline Recap: The Post‑WWII World

  • 1898–1913: War
  • 1898–1900: Military government
  • 1901–1907: Civil government by Presidential Philippine Commission
  • 1907–1912: Philippine Assembly & bicameral legislature
  • 1913–1920: Filipinization (Gov. Harrison)
  • 1921–1926: Rollback of Filipinization (Gov. Wood)
  • 1927–1934: Transition to Commonwealth
  • 1935–1941: U.S.‑Philippine Commonwealth (Quezon)
  • 1941–1944: WWII Japanese occupation
  • 1946: Independence granted

Disputed West PH Sea reef: Manila slams ‘irresponsible’ Chinese report

The Philippines has strongly denied a Chinese state media report claiming that China’s coast guard seized control of Sandy Cay near Thitu Island (Pag‑asa)...

The Philippines has strongly denied a Chinese state media report claiming that China’s coast guard seized control of Sandy Cay, a disputed reef near Thitu Island (Pag‑asa) in the West Philippine Sea.

Tensions between Manila and Beijing have escalated over the South China Sea, where China claims nearly the entire area despite an international ruling rejecting its claims.

Meanwhile, joint US‑Philippines military exercises (Balikatan) involve large‑scale drills including integrated air and missile defense simulations. The United States’ growing military presence and activities in the region have drawn sharp criticism from Beijing, which accuses Manila of "colluding with outside powers" and warns that the exercises threaten regional stability.

China seizes disputed WPS reef near key military outpost – report

According to Chinese state media, China’s coast guard seized control of Sandy Cay (Tiexian Reef), a disputed sandbank near the Philippine military base on Thitu Island...

According to Chinese state media, China’s coast guard seized control of Sandy Cay (Tiexian Reef), a disputed sandbank near the Philippine military base on Thitu Island in the West Philippine Sea. China claims it is exercising sovereignty by inspecting and planting a flag in the area; however, there is no sign of permanent occupation.

This event follows the recent increase in tensions resulting from the Philippines and the US conducting their Balikatan joint military drills. China claims that this act by the United States is destabilizing the region. Both Beijing and Manila have recently blamed each other for environmental damage in contested areas.

US to deploy missiles to Batanes for drills

As covered last week, the US is deploying its NMESIS missile launchers to Batanes and other parts of northern Luzon during Balikatan exercises...

As covered in the previous week, the United States is deploying its NMESIS (Navy‑Marine Expeditionary Ship Interdiction System) missile launchers to Batanes and other parts of northern Luzon during this year's Balikatan military exercises with the Philippines.

However, former Batanes Rep. Florencio "Butch" Abad warned that the deployment could endanger Batanes and its residents (the Ivatans), as the province is geographically close to Taiwan.

He expressed concerns that in the event of a Chinese invasion of Taiwan, Batanes could suffer significant collateral damage, given the critical importance of the nearby Bashi Channel for global trade and the potential for military conflict involving sophisticated weaponry.

This shows how innocent residents could be caught mid‑conflict, which has been evident since the colonization of the Philippines.
